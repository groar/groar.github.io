Diving deep into Curry-Howard.

My research work belongs to the field of proof theory and Curry-Howard correspondance. More precisely: linear logic, forcing and classical realizability and what they can bring to functional programming. Recently I have been interested in the connections between deep learning and functional programming.

PhD Thesis

I did my PhD at the LIPN, Université Paris 13, under the supervision of Damiano Mazza.

The purpose of this thesis is the definition of a new algebraic framework called Monitoring Algebra, used to study complex functional programming languages and their associated correctness results. This framework arises from a dissection of linear variants of Cohen forcing and Krivine realizability under the prism of the Curry-Howard correspondance. This theory allows one to explore and combine new programming features and their associated properties (such as memory consumption, time complexity, differential privacy, etc.).
